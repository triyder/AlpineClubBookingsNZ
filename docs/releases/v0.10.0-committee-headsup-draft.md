## Draft message to the committee

**Subject: Heads-up — a fairness fix to how cancellation refunds work with
account credit**

Kia ora committee,

A small but real change to how booking cancellations are handled has gone into
the booking system, and I want you to know about it before any member asks.

**The short version:** members who pay for a booking using their **account
credit** are now treated the same as members who pay by **card** when they
cancel. Our cancellation policy already keeps a percentage of the payment when
someone cancels close to the date (the "cancellation tiers"). Until now, that
percentage was only applied to card payments — if a member paid with credit and
cancelled late, they got **all** of their credit back, even inside the window
where a card payer would have forfeited it.

**What changes:** the cancellation tier now applies to credit the same way it
applies to card. In particular, **a member who paid for a booking entirely with
account credit and cancels inside the 0%-refund window now gets nothing back** —
exactly what would happen to a member who had paid the same amount by card.

**Why we did it:** it was an unfair loophole. Two members cancelling the same
booking at the same late moment were getting different outcomes purely because
of how they had paid. A member holding credit could always dodge a
late-cancellation penalty by paying with credit. Closing that keeps the policy
consistent and predictable for everyone.

**Is anyone worse off in practice?** Only in the specific case above: a
credit-paying member who cancels inside a penalty window. They now lose the same
share a card payer would have. Members who cancel early enough to be inside a
full-refund tier are unaffected — their credit is still restored in full. Nothing
changes about the cancellation tiers or notice periods themselves; only the
even-handed application of them.

**A related tidy-up (for completeness):** there was a rare edge case where a
booking had been part-refunded by an earlier change and was then cancelled. The
system used to either give the member back nothing, or (if an operator ran a
maintenance tool) everything — neither of which was a deliberate policy choice.
That is now handled properly: the member receives the correct cancellation tier
of whatever value is left, and the maintenance tool no longer decides refunds on
its own — an operator has to confirm each one.

**What we need from members:** nothing. The member-facing cancellation help text
and the cancellation confirmation emails already describe the tiers. There is no
action for members to take.

**What I'd like from you:** a quick nod that you're comfortable with this being
the club's cancellation stance, and a heads-up to me if you think a member is
likely to query it so we can answer consistently.

Happy to walk anyone through the detail.

Ngā mihi,
[owner name]
